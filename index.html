A Letter for Cayla

Cayla,

nineteen. an age that still carries the fragrance of youth, yet already bears the weight of the future. it is the threshold where questions begin to outnumber certainties, and where every step feels both fragile and immense.

in the brief years that i have known you, i have come to see how rare it is for someone so young to possess both clarity and depth. you remind me that strength is not in loudness, but in the quiet persistence of one's will; that maturity is not measured by years, but by the way a person confronts life.

i cannot say much about the world ahead of you. it is vast, often cruel, often kind—but i can say this: i am grateful. grateful that in the strange crossing of paths, i have encountered your existence. to know you is to be reminded that life is richer than i sometimes believe, that there are people whose very being sharpens and steadies those around them.

may your nineteenth year be not only a passage of time, but a widening of soul. may you continue to walk with the same resolve, and may the world, in its chaos, still make room for the music, the clarity, and the light you carry.

Happy nineteenth, Cayla.
